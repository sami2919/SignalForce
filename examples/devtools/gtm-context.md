## Product

**Core offering:** Unified developer productivity platform that reduces friction across the engineering lifecycle — from onboarding to deployment.

- Connects to your existing tools (GitHub, Jira, Slack, CI/CD) and surfaces bottlenecks across the SDLC
- **Key differentiator:** Actionable metrics tied directly to developer workflows, not vanity DORA stats
- Includes workflow automation to eliminate repetitive toil without requiring a dedicated platform team
- Covers onboarding, code review, CI/CD, and deployment stages within a single visibility layer

## Voice & Tone

**Persona:** Fellow engineering leader, not a sales rep.

**Always do:**
- Lead with a specific productivity or process pain point, not product features
- Reference their GitHub activity, job postings, or tech stack directly
- Use engineering-credible language (DORA metrics, toil, cognitive load, SDLC, golden path)
- Keep initial outreach to 4 sentences maximum
- Use low-friction CTAs ("worth a 20-minute conversation?")

**Never use:**
- "I hope this email finds you well"
- Generic productivity statistics without grounding in their context
- "I noticed your company"
- Vague promises like "10x your developer productivity"

## Quick Reference: Signal Keywords

Use these to identify qualified prospects from public signals:

**Strong signals:**
- Platform engineering, developer experience, DevEx, internal developer platform
- Backstage, monorepo tooling, golden path, developer portal
- Job postings for: Platform Engineer, DevEx Engineer, Internal Tools Engineer
- GitHub repos with developer-tools, devex, cli, platform-engineering topics

**Weak / disqualifying signals:**
- Fewer than 15 engineers
- No internal platform or tooling function
- Engineering team is entirely outsourced
