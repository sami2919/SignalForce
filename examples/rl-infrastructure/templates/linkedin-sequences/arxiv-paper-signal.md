# ArXiv Paper Signal LinkedIn Sequence

## Signal Context

A published RL paper signals a team that is actively researching, not just applying off-the-shelf methods. Researchers are reachable on LinkedIn and respond well to peers who have read their work. The connection request must demonstrate genuine engagement with the paper — generic "loved your research" openers fail. Lead with a specific observation or question about the paper's approach.

## Placeholders

- `{{contact_first_name}}` — Recipient's first name
- `{{company_name}}` — Company name
- `{{signal_reference}}` — Paper title (short form) or ArXiv ID
- `{{paper_contribution}}` — Key technical contribution of the paper (one phrase)
- `{{rl_domain}}` — The RL domain the paper addresses (e.g., reward shaping, sim-to-real)

---

## Connection Request Note (≤300 chars)

### Variant A: Signal Reference

Read your {{signal_reference}} paper — the {{paper_contribution}} framing is one of the cleaner takes on {{rl_domain}} I've seen. Would like to connect with researchers working at this depth.

### Variant B: Mutual Interest

Working on {{rl_domain}} infrastructure and found your {{signal_reference}} work genuinely useful. Always glad to connect with people pushing the state of the art here rather than just applying it.

---

## Follow-Up Message (Day 2-3 after connection accepted)

{{contact_first_name}}, appreciate the connect. Following up on your {{signal_reference}} work — curious how {{company_name}} is handling the gap between your {{paper_contribution}} approach and production environment fidelity. We've been compiling case studies on that transition; happy to share what we've found if it's relevant to where you're taking the research.

---

## Second Follow-Up (Day 7, only if no response)

{{contact_first_name}}, sharing a resource either way: a technical write-up on scaling {{rl_domain}} experiments without sacrificing environment reproducibility. Reach out if the timing ever works.
