# Meeting Follow-Up Email Sequences

Placeholders: {{contact_first_name}}, {{company_name}}, {{meeting_date}}, {{objections}}, {{next_steps}}, {{resources_discussed}}

---

## Positive Outcome

> Use when: prospect showed clear interest, asked specific questions, or requested a proposal/next step.

### Email 1 — Day 0: Thank you + resources + next step

**Subject:** Resources from our call + next step

{{contact_first_name}} — appreciate your time today. As promised, [resource addressing {{objections}}] is attached. For {{next_steps}}, I've sent a calendar link — [CTA: grab 30 min here].

### Email 2 — Day 3: Case study relevant to their use case

**Subject:** How [peer company] solved [their specific challenge]

{{contact_first_name}} — thought this was relevant to what you're building at {{company_name}}: [case study link]. Key stat: [specific result tied to their use case]. Happy to walk through the details if useful.

### Email 3 — Day 7: Internal alignment check-in

**Subject:** Re: next steps

{{contact_first_name}} — checking in as you align internally. If it would help to present to [{{stakeholders_needed}}] directly, I'm happy to join. Otherwise, let me know what's most useful from your end.

---

## Neutral Outcome

> Use when: prospect was politely engaged but showed no clear buying signal or urgency.

### Email 1 — Day 0: Thank you + key takeaway + resource

**Subject:** One thing from our conversation

{{contact_first_name}} — good talking through {{company_name}}'s approach on {{meeting_date}}. One resource that maps to your situation: [link from {{resources_discussed}}]. No action needed — just thought it was relevant.

### Email 2 — Day 5: New insight relevant to their situation

**Subject:** New data point for {{company_name}}

{{contact_first_name}} — came across this [benchmark / report / case study] that's directly relevant to [their stated challenge]. Sharing in case it's useful as you think through next steps.

### Email 3 — Day 14: Gentle check-in with new angle

**Subject:** Quick question

{{contact_first_name}} — has anything shifted on your end since we spoke? We've seen [new development / customer story] that may change the calculus. Worth a quick call if timing is better now.

---

## Negative / Not Now

> Use when: prospect explicitly said no, wrong timing, or no budget. No follow-up sequence — add to 6-month re-qualification list.

### Email 1 — Day 0: Thank you, one resource, no pressure

**Subject:** Thanks for your time

{{contact_first_name}} — appreciate the honesty on {{meeting_date}}. No pressure at all — if circumstances change, I'm here. One resource in the meantime that may be useful regardless: [link]. Good luck with [their stated initiative].

*Add to 6-month re-qualification list. No further automated follow-up.*

---

## No-Show

> Use when: prospect missed the scheduled meeting with no prior notice.

### Email 1 — Day 0: No worries + resource share

**Subject:** Missed you — resource I was going to share

{{contact_first_name}} — no worries at all, things happen. I was going to share [resource from {{resources_discussed}}] — it's attached so you have it regardless. Happy to reschedule when timing works.

### Email 2 — Day 3: Reschedule offer + self-serve option

**Subject:** Re: [meeting topic]

{{contact_first_name}} — wanted to make it easy to reconnect: [calendar link] if a call still makes sense, or [self-serve resource / demo video] if you'd rather explore async. Either way, happy to help when you're ready.
